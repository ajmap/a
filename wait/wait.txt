(8) the door to peace opens. Remember Jesus sleeping in the boat amid the storm. The wind roared, the waves crashed, the disciples trembled, but he rested. That is being without hurry. That is knowing that even though the world is shaken, God rules. That is having the soul anchored in the center, not on the surface of events. When I am without hurry, time stops squeezing me. It neither lengthens nor shortens. It fills with presence. And in that presence, everything flows with more clarity. What once seemed urgent no longer is. What seemed a problem dissolves. What seemed an obstacle becomes a teacher. Because those who know how to be without hurry know how to see beyond. Once in New York, I was in a very long line to send a letter. In front of me, a man complained non-stop. Every 30 seconds , he checked his watch. He murmured, sighed, fidgeted. Beside him, an older woman remained silent. Her face was serene, almost smiling. Curious, I asked, "How can you be so calm with such a wait?" She replied softly, "I am alive, aren't I? This moment belongs to me, too. I am here and that is enough. I never forgot that answer because that woman had understood something essential. Being is not wasting time. Being is gaining depth. But the world does not teach this. The world teaches that doing is more important than being. That going is better than staying. That arriving is more important than living the journey. And so many run and run and keep running. Until one day they ask themselves, "Where was I going? What part of my soul was left behind while running? I am not called to that. I am called to remember. To remember that I am already whole. That I don't need to run to have worth. That life is not a competition but an expression of being. And that the kingdom of God is reached not by speed but by presence. That is why in every moment I must wait. I say to myself, "This moment is not an obstacle. It is a teacher. I don't have to hurry. I only have to be." And as I say this, I will feel my soul settle, my body relax, my mind unwind. And then begins the art. The art of being fully where I am without fighting the clock, without resisting the hour. When I am truly present without urgency, I begin to notice things I didn't see before. The kind gesture of a stranger, the architecture of a building, the rhythm of my heart, the beauty of shared silence, the gentle voice of the spirit saying, "All is well." And then I understand waiting is not the empty space between two important things. Waiting is life itself wanting to teach me how to be. There is no greater gift than this. Knowing I don't have to run. That good is already on its way. That God is already working. That I am already whole. And this very moment is enough. Practical exercise. Being where I am. This simple exercise will help me cultivate the art of being without hurry. I can do it anywhere. In a line, on a train car, in a waiting room, or even at home. One, I stop physically. I sit down or stand still, but I stop. I do nothing else for a moment. Two, I bring my attention to my body. I feel my feet on the ground, my hands, the rhythm of my breathing. Three, I say mentally, "I am here. I don't have to go anywhere right now. This moment is enough. I am complete." Four, I observe without judgment. I look around. I listen to the sounds. I don't name them. I just receive them. Five, if hurry appears, I don't fight it. I say hello. Hurry, I don't need you anymore. I am in God. Six, I stay like this for 2 or 3 minutes. Then if I want, I continue what I was doing, but with a different energy. I do this every day during every wait. And I will see how without realizing it, I begin to live from another dimension. Not from the clock, not from obligation, but from being. And being does not run. Being simply is. And in that being, everything is revealed. 

Chapter 11 

Waiting as a creative act

Waiting is not pacivity. Waiting is not in action. Waiting in its highest sense is creating. Yes, you read that right. Waiting is a creative act. But only when you wait in God. Only when you wait with faith, with purpose, with awakened awareness. I'm not talking about resigned waiting nor impatient waiting. Nor waiting that becomes desperate. I speak of waiting as a spiritual art, a sacred moment in which the soul collaborates with the universe without anxiety, without resistance, without hurry. Because every true wait is a pregnancy of the soul. Something is being formed in the silence. Something invisible settles deep inside. And when I know how to wait with peace, I help shape that manifestation. My faith is the womb. My attention is the matrix. My perseverance is the heartbeat that nourishes the process. And what happens when I don't wait properly? The same as opening an oven too soon, the bread is ruined. The same as picking an unripe fruit from the tree, it turns bitter. But if I wait with understanding, without demanding, without pushing, without forcing, then waiting becomes creating. Faith is the assurance of things hoped for. That phrase contains this entire chapter. Faith is not an emotion. It is a certainty. And that certainty has a direction. Waiting. Whoever has faith knows how to wait. Whoever does not know how to wait has not awakened faith. And that is why impatience disguised as hope is confused for faith. Let me say it clearly. When I wait anxiously, I am sewing confusion. When I wait complaining, I am affirming lack. When I wait doubting, I delay manifestation. But when I wait with love, I am creating with God. I once wrote, I'm in the middle of a very difficult situation. I know I need faith, but nothing happens. I pray, I affirm, I wait, but the solution doesn't come. What do I do? I answer with just one line. Am I waiting with faith or am I waiting for the waiting to end? Later, I thank myself. I can say I understand something profound, that waiting time is gestation time, not punishment, and that I was rushing the process with my impatience, like someone yelling at a seed, "Hurry up and grow." And that of course does not work. Because everything that is from God has its perfect time, not human time, but divine time, the time when the soul is ready, the time when the vibration is aligned, the time when form does not hinder the essence. Waiting as a creative act means participating with my mind and heart in the invisible work being done. It is not sitting and staring at the clock. It is sitting in my inner temple and saying, "God, thank you because you have already heard my prayer. Even though I do not see it yet, I know it is done. This wait is not emptiness. It is fullness on its way." And then I stand firm without wavering, without complaining, without seeking proof, without manipulating. And then what the ancients called a miracle happens. The external manifestation begins to move. People appear. Solutions arise. Paths open. Ideas descend. But all this comes from the hidden work I did in the wait. I remember this. The sewer who plants his seed in the ground does not dig every hour to see if it's growing. He knows. He trusts. He waters. He protects. He waits. And that waiting is an act of love. That waiting is silent creation. So too, when I affirm a truth, when I do a treatment, when I visualize from faith, my part is to hold that vibration while the universe arranges the visible. That is my responsibility. Not to create the outcome, not to control the how, but to wait with an active soul. I always wait with peace. I do not rush when called to heal. I am not impatient when my mind does not understand. I do not force his hour. I know that in God everything happens when it must happen. And that is why I say, "My time has not yet come." (9) I too have a spiritual time for everything. I do not hurry. I do not stop. I just wait with presence. And when the ego rises with its old script, what if it doesn't happen? What if it takes too long? What if it's too late? What if it wasn't meant for me? I simply breathe and respond. I am waiting with God. And with God, nothing can go wrong.

Practical exercise. Waiting by creating.

This exercise will train me to wait with creative awareness, especially in situations where I cannot speed up the outcome. One, I identify a situation I am waiting for, a response, a change, a healing, a job, a reconciliation. Two, I sit quietly, I close me eyes, breathe slowly, and I become aware of your body. Three, I visualize that situation already resolved. I don't force it, I just feel it. I imagine how I feel in that future already realized. No external details, just the deep feeling. Four, I affirm quietly to myself, "Thank you, God, because it is already done. While I wait, I create. While I do not see it, I strengthen it in my faith. This wait is part of the manifestation." Five. I open my eyes and I smile, not with tense hope, but with calm certainty. I do this every day, even for a minute and I will see how waiting fills with meaning, because I will no longer be waiting for something. I will be creating with my wait. And that is the greatest power of the awakened soul. Knowing that love is not coming, love is already here. It is only waiting for me to wait for it. Like waiting for the sun, with faith, with peace, with gratitude. 

Chapter 12

Waiting as perpetual prayer. 

Most people pray at specific moments. They pray when something hurts, when something is missing, when fear appears or hope surfaces, and then return to their affairs like someone making a quick call, waiting for a response to come later. But the highest truth is not reached through occasional prayers. It is reached when life itself becomes a constant prayer. When every thought, every action, every silence and every wait is an ongoing conversation with God. In this way, waiting ceases to be a nuisance. It becomes perpetual prayer. It is not about endlessly repeating words nor living with artificial solemnity. It is about being in communion. It is about staying connected to the source while the world spins, while I wait my turn, while everything seems slower than I wish. When I turn waiting into prayer, I am no longer waiting for something to happen outside. I am remembering who I am inside. I am reaffirming my faith. I am breathing the presence of Christ within me. This is the state I call watching and praying. I don't say to repeat, please without stopping. I say to remain awake in the awareness of God. And waiting is the perfect ground for that awakening. Why? Because it stops me, puts me face to face with time, confronts my impatience, and then if I choose, leads me to silence. And silence, when inhabited by faith, is one of the highest forms of prayer. Many have asked, "How can I pray all day if I have responsibilities? How do I turn a line, a waiting room, a traffic jam into prayer? Isn't that impossible amid noise and distractions?" My answer is always the same. It's not about changing what I do. It's about changing where I do it from. It's about praying with my attitude, my vibration, my breath. I'm in line at the bank. I breathe deeply. I feel the life in my body. I think God is here. That thought is already prayer. Am I waiting for news? I place my hands on my heart. I mentally say, "What is coming is already blessed." That affirmation is already prayer. Am I waiting for someone who hasn't arrived? I look at the sky. I look at my hands. I say, "In this moment, I lack nothing. I am." And there I am praying. Prayer is not an act. It's a state. A state of connection, surrender, alignment. A state in which I do not talk about God or talk to God, but speak from God. And that can be done, must be done in every waiting. And I think of the sundial. It has no hands, makes no sound. It only marks the hour when light touches it. So should be the soul that waits, still open, receptive, full of expectant silence. When I wait like this, I am praying because my mind is centered, my body is present, and my heart is at peace. That is perpetual prayer. It does not ask, it does not demand. It knows God acts, knows life manifests, knows good reveals itself. And because it knows, it does not need to be restless. It simply waits with love. And what happens when I pray like this? Everything changes. Not because the world changes, but because I have changed. And as in the law, my world is a reflection of my consciousness. When I pray while I wait, I bless my surroundings. People calm down. The atmosphere softens. Solutions accelerate. Answers appear. Not by magic, but because the presence of God, when expressed through me, orders and heals what it touches. A woman wrote to me. I have transformed my life by turning waiting into prayer. Before I got impatient about everything now when I wait for something, a message, a call, a person, I don't get upset. I breathe. I say, "I am praying with my waiting." And suddenly everything comes easier. And if it doesn't come, I am still at peace. That woman understood the power I also have because I don't need a special place, free time, or a burning candle to pray. I only need to choose to live from the soul, not from the rush. And in waiting the soul has its space because while the body is held back, the spirit can act. I withdraw to solitary places to pray. Yes. But I also pray while walking, pray while speaking, pray while healing, learning, living. My life is a perpetual prayer. And I am called to the same. To live so that wherever I am, whatever I do, God's presence is felt in my way of seeing, waiting, responding. And this is not for saints on display. This is for me here and now. The next time I find myself in a line, a delay, a forced pause, I say within myself, I am praying with my attitude. I am praying with my breath. This wait is my temple. God is here and so am I. I make that wait an altar. An altar without incense, without flowers, without hymns, but full of living presence. Because wherever I remember God, there is a house of prayer. Even if it's a train station, a bench, a hospital, a sidewalk, there eternity touches time and transforms it. Practical exercise. The prayer of waiting. This exercise is simple but profound. It involves uniting breath with awareness to turn waiting into active prayer. I choose a short affirmation. It could be God is here. I am at peace. I trust in good. Everything is in divine order. While waiting, sitting, standing, walking, I do this. I inhale gently and mentally say the first part. For example, God is. Exhale slowly and complete the phrase. For example, here. I repeat this for a few minutes. I don't count or control. I just breathe, affirm, and listen. If I get distracted, I smile, and return. Distraction does not cancel prayer. It's part of the training. When finished, I say inside, "Thank you for this wordless prayer. Thank you for reminding me that waiting is also loving." And so, chapter by chapter, I have learned to transform waiting. It is no longer punishment, no longer annoyance, no longer emptiness. Now it is prayer. Now it is art. Now it is creation. Now it is presence. And above all now it is a path. Because every moment I wait from the soul is one more step toward the kingdom of God which comes not with announcements, hurry or noise but with silence, love and a single certainty. I am with me always to the very end of the age. And in that perpetual waiting, me and God wait.
