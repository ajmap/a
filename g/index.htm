I'm going on a pleasant walk. I'm on a flat soft path covered with soft tree bark. The path winds back and forth and ahead I see a tall hedge, there is an opening in the hedge and the path leads through this opening. As I slowly walk through the opening in the hedge, I see the most beautiful garden I have ever seen. There is a profusion of flowers and it is laid out in perfection in geometric designs that are pleasing to my eyes. I slowly gaze around and observe the galaxy of flowers. Some have bright colors some have subdued colors, but they are all beautiful, absolutely gorgeous, there are flowers of all kinds and colors. Reds, deep vermilion, delicate pinks. There are blue flowers, deep blue, cerulean blue, azure blue, greenish blue. There are vivid yellows and spring greens, dark green and some olive colored flowers, all colors of the rainbow, all shades. Greens, reds, yellows, blues and purples, oranges and violets. Some flowers have large petals, broad and smoothly shaped like a ships hull. Some flowers are tiny and intricate, they appear delicate, but somehow I know they are hardy. I see such detail in this tiny flowers, such beautiful minute petals. Being in this garden is an inspiration, being here in this garden fills me with awe, the beauty is overwhelming, being here fills me with the desire to create. Such a garden should be enjoyed by all, being in this realm of truth and beauty inspires me to write a poem or write a story or paint a picture, express what I feel in some way. Some way that is right for me. I will find a way to express myself, happily, effortlessly, joyfully. Each of these flowers represent something to me, some represent an idea or a concept, some of the flowers represent parts of a story or parts of a beautiful picture. Some of the flowers represent parts of a speech or a sermon. Other represent acts that I can do for the good of others. These flowers are limitless. The more I pick, the more they thrive and grow. I may pick as many flowers as I wish. Go ahead and pick some bouquets, I pick large bunches, I may pick many bunches if I wish. I can always come back for more. They will always be here. Winter, spring, summer, fall, these flowers grow all year, they are waiting for me to pick them, they want me to pick them because that is what they're here for, my benefit, for my good, to help me fulfill my creative urges. Now carefully I tuck my bouquets under my arms, I hold some in my hands, I may even put some flowers in my hair if I desire. I hold them gently, for they are precious. They are my desires.
