Because when the soul aligns, life organizes itself. When consciousness calms, time becomes a friend. This is the secret the world has forgotten. It's not about managing time. Time well understood is just a way that God manifests and when I live with God at the center, I live in just the right time. Neither before nor after, but exactly when it should be. Then there is no guilt for the past nor anxiety for the future. Only a quiet certainty. And that certainty does not come from the calendar, the clock or control. It comes from a soul that has made this single powerful decision to put God first. 

Chapter 12

Revealed purpose
   
When God occupies the center, everything else finds its place. For this I was born and for this I have come into the world. The human soul, even amid all its searches, longs for one thing. Meaning. What have I come here to do? And so many begin a path of external searching, processions, titles, missions, causes. Some find it partially. Others believe they have found it and then lose it. But the truth is that no true purpose can be revealed without first ordering the consciousness. And consciousness is only ordered when God occupies the center. Purpose is not something invented. It is not a goal defined rationally or a role chosen like a suit. Purpose is a revelation, a flame that ignites when the soul has been purified of distractions, masks, and foreign ambitions. And that purification begins with one single decision to give God the place God deserves. When the spirit is first, everything else begins to find its place. Confusion dissipates. The voices of the world quiet down. Haste relinquishes its throne. And in that fertile silence, something emerges from deep within. A soft but unshakable certainty, a direction that does not come from analysis, but from the soul itself. For this I was born. Purpose doesn't always shout. Sometimes it whispers. Sometimes it manifests in small hints, repeated affinities, and unmistakable peace when doing a certain task. But what sets it apart from any other path is that it doesn't drain, it renews. It doesn't demand. It expands. It doesn't trap, it frees. I never speak of purpose as a burden. I lived it as expression. I am not seeking to be someone. I am manifesting what I already am because I know where I come from (8) and where I am going. And that knowledge does not come from study but from my perfect union with God. That's why I say, "I and the father are one." And from that humility, every word, every step, every gesture flows naturally as the fulfillment of my purpose. This is what happens when God is put first. Purpose is not sought, it is remembered. It is not manufactured, it is allowed, it is not conquered, it is embodied. The soul stops copying foreign paths, comparing itself, wondering if it is doing the right thing because it no longer acts from the small eye, but from the I am that dwells at its center. Then purpose may be grand in the eyes of the world or go unnoticed. But it doesn't matter because it is not measured by recognition but by alignment. And what is aligned is already fulfilling its reason for being. I make it a habit. Before deciding what to do with my life, I ask first, who occupies the center of my consciousness? If it is fear, I will be pursuing security. If it is ambition, I will be pursuing approval. If it is comparison, I will be running after what doesn't belong to me. But if it is God, then whatever I do will be an expression of my truth. And that truth needs no justification. It only needs to be lived. My purpose is not something I need to invent with anguish. It is a frequency that reveals itself when everything else has been silenced. It is a music that already plays within me, but can only be heard when God is the conductor. And once I hear that melody, I can no longer return to the noise. I can no longer settle for living by inertia. I can no longer ignore the fire that has awakened because the soul that has put God first no longer needs to search outside. It has found its reason for being within. And from there, everything falls into place. Everything makes sense. Everything blossoms effortlessly. 

Chapter 13 

Inner obedience

Stopping resistance is beginning to receive. He who has my commandments and keeps them. It is God who loves me and I will manifest myself to God. In the language of the spirit, obedience is not submitting to an external will. It is not obeying orders out of fear, nor forcing external behavior to comply with rigid rules. To obey in the consciousness of the kingdom is to align oneself internally with the law of good. It is to stop resisting the divine direction that already flows in the soul and allow it to occupy the place it deserves, the first place. Resistance does not always show itself as open rebellion. Often it disguises itself as control, excessive analysis, the desire to have guarantees before taking a step or clinging to old beliefs and emotions that no longer serve, to paths that the heart knows are not right. And all that resistance, although it may seem protective, is the barrier preventing us from receiving what the spirit wants to deliver. Because God is always giving good. The law is always flowing. The law is always active, but it cannot force its way into a soul that insists on directing from the ego. Inner obedience is not blind. It is intelligent. It is profound. It is an act of sacred humility that says, "I don't need to understand everything to trust. I don't need to have the complete map to move forward." If God is first in my consciousness, then his direction is secure and my surrender is wise. Jesus himself lived it. The master did not act out of personal will. He did not seek fame, approval or results. His power came from his perfect obedience to the spirit. And that obedience was not weakness. It was spiritual authority. Because those who have surrendered to the higher law can walk with power over the waters of life. Resistance, on the other hand, wears us down. It makes the path feel hard, the decisions heavy, and the days pass without clear direction because those who do not obey the spirit obey the world. And the world changes, demands, and confuses. But the spirit guides with peace, guides with certainty, guides with love. When the soul surrenders inwardly, when it stops fighting, justifying, or negotiating with the voice of God, then the channel of reception is opened. What seemed blocked is freed. What wasn't coming arrives. What hurt heals because there is no more interference. There are no longer two wills in conflict. There is only one mind, one law, one direction, the will of God, and the soul unified with him. This obedience is not passive. It is an active constant loving act. It is saying at every moment your will be done in me. Not out of fear but because I know my will is good. Not out of resignation but because I trust your wisdom more than my limited ideas. And from that surrender my life begins to organize itself effortlessly. My soul no longer wears itself out pushing doors that won't open. It does not blame myself for not understanding the why of each thing. It simply listens, follows, obeys, and receives. I try this. When I feel confused, I don't search for more answers. I search for inner obedience. When I feel resistance, I don't blame myself. I just recognize, "I am trying to direct something that the spirit already knows how to resolve." And then I release, not a drift, but to the center, not by chance, but to the law. And I say, "Goodness guides me, and I obey with joy." Because resistance takes me away, but obedience connects me to wholeness. And step by step, my life stops being a struggle and becomes a clear current. Because true obedience is not slavery. It is inner freedom. And when my soul stops resisting, it is finally ready to receive. 

Chapter 14

God is not part of life. God is life and everything else is added by law. In God I live and move and have my being. But this fragmented view cannot generate peace, abundance or true purpose. Because God is not a portion of existence. God is existence itself. God is not another element on the life's board. God is the entire board, the background, the origin, the substance of everything. The mistake is in separating my spiritual life from other aspects, work, relationships, finances, the body, plans. And so my soul is divided, consciousness weakens, and my spirit loses its axis in the mind of man, though in reality it has never moved. What moves is the attention, what shifts is the priority. Not as an occasional miracle, but as the result of a living, exact and eternal law. When God is placed first, it's not like putting a check mark on a list of priorities. One, God. Two, family. Three, work. Four, health. Five, finances. No, God is the foundation that sustains everything else. God is the field where all the seeds grow. God is the ocean from which all forms emerge and to which they return. If that root is not nourished, the tree does not bear fruit. God is not part of your financial life. God is the source of everything in it. God is not part of your health. God is the intelligence that organizes my cells. God is not part of my relationship. God is the love that unites in harmony and truth. God is not part of my decisions. God is the wisdom that inspires them from above. And when I live from this vision, not as a concept but as a certainty, then everything that seemed complex begins to simplify. Every decision is imbued with clarity. Every relationship reflects truth. And what was once considered material becomes an extension of the spirit. Thus the law is fulfilled. God first and everything else falls into place. God at the center and everything else turns in harmony. God is life and life becomes full, prosperous and guided. There are no exceptions. There is no circumstance that does not respond to this order. Because when I recognize that God is not part of life but life itself, the soul finally rests and in resting it receives. Living in priority. 

The eternal secret is not lost, only forgotten. But one thing is necessary, and Mary has chosen the better part, which will not be taken from her. Jesus of Nazareth. The human soul is not lost. It is in what does not change, the presence, the eternal love, the truth that already is, even though the world has forgotten it. The soul that remembers this priority, even if it doesn't yet know how things will be resolved externally, lives with a piece that doesn't need explanation because it has already chosen the better part. And that key has always been in my hand. It just needed to be remembered.
