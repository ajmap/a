He was manifesting what he already was because he knew where he came from and where he was going. And that knowledge did not come from study but from his perfect union with the father. That's why he said, "I and the father are one." And from that humility, every word, every step, every gesture flowed naturally as the fulfillment of his purpose. This is what happens when God is put first. Purpose is not sought, it is remembered. It is not manufactured, it is allowed, it is not conquered, it is embodied. The soul stops copying foreign paths, comparing itself, wondering if it is doing the right thing because it no longer acts from the small eye, but from the I am that dwells at its center. Then purpose may be grand in the eyes of the world or go unnoticed. But it doesn't matter because it is not measured by recognition but by alignment. And what is aligned is already fulfilling its reason for being. I make it a habit. Before deciding what to do with my life, I ask first, who occupies the center of my consciousness? If it is fear, I will be pursuing security. If it is ambition, I will be pursuing approval. If it is comparison, I will be running after what doesn't belong to me. But if it is God, then whatever I do will be an expression of my truth. And that truth needs no justification. It only needs to be lived. My purpose is not something I need to invent with anguish. It is a frequency that reveals itself when everything else has been silenced. It is a music that already plays within me, but can only be heard when God is the conductor. And once I hear that melody, I can no longer return to the noise. I can no longer settle for living by inertia. I can no longer ignore the fire that has awakened because the soul that has put God first no longer needs to search outside. It has found its reason for being within. And from there, everything falls into place. Everything makes sense. Everything blossoms effortlessly. 

Chapter 13 

Inner obedience

Stopping resistance is beginning to receive. He who has my commandments and keeps them. It is God who loves me and I will manifest myself to God. In the language of the spirit, obedience is not submitting to an external will. It is not obeying orders out of fear, nor forcing external behavior to comply with rigid rules. To obey in the consciousness of the kingdom is to align oneself internally with the law of good. It is to stop resisting the divine direction that already flows in the soul and allow it to occupy the place it deserves, the first place. Resistance does not always show itself as open rebellion. Often it disguises itself as control, excessive analysis, the desire to have guarantees before taking a step or clinging to old beliefs and emotions that no longer serve, to paths that the heart knows are not right. And all that resistance, although it may seem protective, is the barrier preventing us from receiving what the spirit wants to deliver. Because God is always giving good. The law is always flowing. The law is always active, but it cannot force its way into a soul that insists on directing from the ego. Inner obedience is not blind. It is intelligent. It is profound. It is an act of sacred humility that says, "I don't need to understand everything to trust. I don't need to have the complete map to move forward." If God is first in my consciousness, then his direction is secure and my surrender is wise. Jesus himself lived it. The master did not act out of personal will. He did not seek fame, approval or results. His power came from his perfect obedience to the spirit. And that obedience was not weakness. It was spiritual authority. Because those who have surrendered to the higher law can walk with power over the waters of life. Resistance, on the other hand, wears us down. It makes the path feel hard, the decisions heavy, and the days pass without clear direction because those who do not obey the spirit obey the world. And the world changes, demands, and confuses. But the spirit guides with peace, guides with certainty, guides with love. When the soul surrenders inwardly, when it stops fighting, justifying, or negotiating with the voice of God, then the channel of reception is opened. What seemed blocked is freed. What wasn't coming arrives. What hurt heals because there is no more interference. There are no longer two wills in conflict. There is only one mind, one law, one direction, the will of God, and the soul unified with him. This obedience is not passive. It is an active constant loving act. It is saying at every moment your will be done in me. Not out of fear but because I know my will is good. Not out of resignation but because I trust your wisdom more than my limited ideas. And from that surrender my life begins to organize itself effortlessly. My soul no longer wears itself out pushing doors that won't open. It does not blame myself for not understanding the why of each thing. It simply listens, follows, obeys, and receives. I try this. When I feel confused, I don't search for more answers. I search for inner obedience. When I feel resistance, I don't blame myself. I just recognize, "I am trying to direct something that the spirit already knows how to resolve." And then I release, not a drift, but to the center, not by chance, but to the law. And I say, "Goodness guides me, and I obey with joy." Because resistance takes me away, but obedience connects me to wholeness. And step by step, my life stops being a struggle and becomes a clear current. Because true obedience is not slavery. It is inner freedom. And when my soul stops resisting, it is finally ready to receive. 

Chapter 14

God is not part of life. God is life and everything else is added by law. In God I live and move and have my being. The great spiritual misunderstanding of humanity is not that we don't believe in God, but that we relegate him to a part of our life as if the spirit were a room we enter only on Sundays or a resource we turn to in times of crisis. But this fragmented view cannot generate peace, abundance or true purpose. Because God is not a portion of existence. God is existence itself. God is not another element on the life's board. God is the entire board, the background, the origin, the substance of everything. The mistake is in separating my spiritual life from other aspects, work, relationships, finances, the body, plans. And so my soul is divided, consciousness weakens, and my spirit loses its axis in the mind of man, though in reality it has never moved.

What moves is the attention, what shifts is the priority. Jesus did not come to teach me how to include God in my activities. He came to teach me that my whole life is spiritual and only when I recognize God as life itself does everything else fall into place. Not as an occasional miracle, but as the result of a living, exact and eternal law. When God is placed first, it's not like putting a check mark on a list of priorities. One, God. Two, family. Three, work. Four, health. Five, finances. No, God is the foundation that sustains everything else. God is the field where all the seeds grow. God is the ocean from which all forms emerge and to which they return. If that root is not nourished, the tree does not bear fruit. God is not part of your financial life. God is the source of everything in it. God is not part of your health. God is the intelligence that organizes my cells. God is not part of my relationship. God is the love that unites in harmony and truth. God is not part of my decisions. God is the wisdom that inspires them from above. And when I live from this vision, not as a concept but as a certainty, then everything that seemed complex begins to simplify. The burdens lighten, problems change their face because I am no longer resolving from separate parts but living from the unique and total center. This is the great transformation to move from living a fragmented life to living a unified life where each part expresses the whole and the whole is God. That is why Jesus said, "Seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness and all these things will be added to you." He didn't say some or just the spiritual ones. He said all because all that is true. All that is necessary. All that is noble and good flows from the same root. And that root is invisible but powerful. It cannot be touched but it can be felt. It cannot be bought but it sustains real wealth. It is not confined to temples because it dwells in the heart of the awakened soul. I make it a habit when I start the day. I don't ask where can I include God today. Instead I ask how can I remember that everything I do today is in God with God from God. And then every action even if it seems common becomes sacred. Every decision is imbued with clarity. Every relationship reflects truth. And what was once considered material becomes an extension of the spirit. Thus the law is fulfilled. God first and everything else falls into place. God at the center and everything else turns in harmony. God is life and life becomes full, prosperous and guided. There are no exceptions. There is no circumstance that does not respond to this order. Because when I recognize that God is not part of life but life itself, the soul finally rests and in resting it receives. And in receiving it gives, and in giving it returns to the source from which everything comes. 

Chapter 15
     
Living in priority. 

The eternal secret is not lost, only forgotten. But one thing is necessary, and Mary has chosen the better part, which will not be taken from her. Jesus of Nazareth. The human soul is not lost. It is asleep and the secret it seeks has not disappeared. It has only been buried under layers of noise, haste, ego, and forgetfulness. That secret, the oldest yet most relevant, has been repeated, whispered, and written in all languages. But it is only revealed to those who are willing to live in priority. And that priority is not a concept. It is a practice, a daily decision, a re-education of the soul to return to the original axis, God first. From the beginning, all authentic spiritual teachings have pointed to this single point that the cause of all fullness, peace, direction, healing, and prosperity is not found in form, accumulation or external approval, but in the correct relationship with the source. And that relationship only flourishes when it is given the first place, the center, the primacy in consciousness. Mary understood this, but Martha did not. One was busy with many things, believing that doing was the most important. The other sat at the feet of the master, knowing that all right action springs first from being in order, and Jesus did not correct her, he validated her. Mary has chosen the better part, and it will not be taken from her. Because everything that comes from spiritual priority is eternal. While what arises from confusion is transient, even if it seems urgent, living in priority does not mean neglecting my responsibilities. It means performing them from another frequency. It means waking up in the morning and remembering that my first move is not to look at the clock but to look within. It is not about rushing after duty but first aligning with being. It is not planning the day from fear but from certainty. If God is first, everything else will be ordered. This principle repeated in all sacred scriptures does not need to be reinvented. It just needs to be remembered and living it is coming back home. It is turning off the noise of the world without disconnecting from it. It is being in the world but not of the world. It is acting without anxiety, deciding without fragmentation, giving without expecting and receiving without guilt. Living in priority is knowing that when a problem arises, the solution does not begin externally but in returning to the center. If something hurts, the relief does not come from drama, but from sitting in silence with God first and then acting. The secret is not in techniques or methods that change with the times. It is in what does not change, the presence, the eternal love, the truth that already is, even though the world has forgotten it. The soul that remembers this priority, even if it doesn't yet know how things will be resolved externally, lives with a piece that doesn't need explanation because it has already chosen the better part. And that part will not be taken from it. I have read, I have searched. I have longed for answers, and now I have them, not in the new, but in the eternal. God is first, and everything else without exception is added. There is no other path, no other secret, no other power. And when the soul decides to live it, not just as a belief, but as an embodied practice, then the invisible becomes form. Chaos becomes order. And life, all of life, becomes a constant reflection of the love that created everything. Because living in priority is not a spiritual option. It is the key that opens all doors. And that key has always been in my hand. It just needed to be remembered.
