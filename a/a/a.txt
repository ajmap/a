The human mind believes that peace is the result of external conditions. That it will come when there are no problems, when debts are resolved, when conflict disappears, when others change, when health is perfect, or when everything is in its place. But the peace I speak of does not depend on the world. It is prior to the world. It is above the world. It is immune to what happens in the world. True peace is an internal state that arises from a single cause, a consciousness centered in God. It is not obtained by struggling, by thinking more, or by controlling the details. It is revealed when the mind stops resisting what is and surrenders with trust to the principle that holds everything together. When God is first, peace is not a goal. It is a natural fruit. Just as the tree does not force its fruit, but produces it by law when it is well-rooted, the mind also bears its noblest fruit, peace, when it is rooted in truth. And what is the truth? That God is present, that the source is not withdrawn, that the spirit reigns, that I am not alone or abandoned in a hostile universe. This understanding held steadily transforms mental noise into clarity, conflict into harmony, tension into inner rest. Peace does not come as a response to what happens but as a reflection of how one thinks. A fragmented consciousness divided between fear and faith, between the desire to control and the longing to surrender cannot be peace because peace only dwells in the one.
