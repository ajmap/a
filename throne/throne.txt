The legitimate throne. Recognizing my divine right to reign in my own world. 

One of the greatest obstacles to human fulfillment is the forgetting of the true spiritual position that each soul occupies in the divine plan. Since ancient times, I have been taught, often subliminally, to see myself as small, unworthy, subordinate to chance, and as powerless victims of external forces. But this is a great falsehood, a falsehood that has kept wandering souls in the deserts of doubt, resignation, and fear. The glorious and eternal truth is this. Not with tyranny, not with violence, but with the serene and loving authority of one who knows they are the rightful heir to the kingdom of God. When the inner spirit speaks of the kingdom of heaven, I am not talking about a far-off place after death. I am speaking of a state of consciousness that can and must manifest here and now. It is the conscious recognition that as a child of the source, I have a divine right to live in harmony, abundance, and freedom. Sitting on my legitimate throne means humbly but firmly accepting my role as co-creator with God. It means ceasing to crawl in the dust of guilt, fear, or false humility. It means honoring the dignity that was given to me before the world was made. It's not about arrogance. It's not about an inflated ego seeking to dominate or stand out. It's about remembering that I and God are one in essence. That my life is not an accident. That my very existence is a living proclamation of God's will. Many fear assuming this internal throne because they have been taught that recognizing their own spiritual greatness is a sin. But is it a sin to accept the gifts of God? Is it arrogance to honor the creator by living up to his work? To deny my right to reign in my world is ultimately a lack of faith. A denial of the eternal promise that good, peace, and glory are my natural inheritance. How to recognize my legitimate throne? When I abdicate my internal authority, chaos, confusion, and fear take over my kingdom. When I reclaim my throne, peace and order begin to be restored. Second, I must understand that reigning is not about controlling, but about governing with spiritual wisdom. It's not about forcing circumstances to obey me. It's about elevating my consciousness until external circumstances reflect my internal state of harmony. Third, I must remember that my throne does not depend on external approval. It doesn't matter if the world recognizes me or not. (5) My spiritual royalty doesn't need crowns or applause. It is an invisible, powerful, inviable reality sustained by God himself. What does it mean practically speaking to sit on my throne? It means taking full responsibility for my inner life, stopping the blame of others, the past, the system, or fate. It means recognizing that although I may not always control external events, I can govern my reactions, my attitudes, my interpretations. It means choosing each day thoughts that build, emotions that strengthen, words that bless, and actions that create. It means decreeing with spiritual authority, I am the legitimate sovereign of my world. By the power of God in me, I decree peace, order, and prosperity in my life. I be like a good king or queen, watchful, wise, firm in love. I remember the inner spirit in me as the legitimate king, not a king of this material world, but a spiritual sovereign who rules with justice, compassion, and truth. When I recognize myself as the legitimate child of the most high, I no longer need to compete, compare, or beg for love or acceptance, I walk with dignity, I speak with authority, I act with confidence, and inevitably my outer world begins to reflect that inner order. Life responds to the one who knows who they are. Circumstances align with the consciousness that generates them. The throne is not a physical place. It is a state of consciousness. And that state is available to me right now. Not when I am more perfect, not when I deserve it more, but now because I am already worthy by being a child of God. Today, if I hear this call, I can claim my throne. I don't have to wait for someone to grant it to me. It has already been given to me. I receive it with gratitude. I sit on it with reverence. I rule my world with love and watch as everything around me begins to reflect the silent majesty of one who knows they were created to reign not in arrogance but in service, not to exalt myself, but to manifest the glory of God in my daily life. Thus I will reign not by force but by the invincible power of the spirit within me and my kingdom will be firm, prosperous and eternal because it will be founded upon the living rock of the spirit within.

The scripture expresses this powerfully. I stand firm and do not be moved from the hope of the gospel. This firmness is not rigidity, not human stubbornness. Every time I remember this, every time I affirm internally, my good does not come from outside, my good comes from God in me. I strengthen the invisible roots that sustain me. It is a current of life that must be fed every day with thoughts of faith, actions of coherence, and decisions of love. I must watch over the soil of my consciousness every day. I pull up the weeds as soon as they appear. Water my thoughts with constant affirmations. How do I protect the mind? Every morning upon awakening, I set my intention clearly. Today, I remain in the peace of God. Today I trust in the good that unfolds in my life. Today I am stable, serene and secure because my foundation is the spirit. And every night before bed do a loving review of your day. What thoughts built my day? Where did I allow fear to whisper? When I acknowledge the source, I root myself more deeply in it. And when I am rooted in God, nothing external can shake me. I make gratitude a habit, not an occasional reaction. Because I know I am not there by chance. Because I know I do not depend on external circumstances. Because I know my life is sustained, nourished and celebrated by life itself. Thus I will stay. Thus I will grow even more. Thus I will bear abundant fruit and my joy will be complete. Not because I am perfect, not because I never falter, but because I have learned to anchor myself. Not in myself, but in the infinite love that created me, sustains me, and crowns me with eternal blessings. So it is, so it has always been, and so it will always be.

I have been called to build not a temporary hut but a spiritual palace, a firm throne where Christ in me reigns in peace, joy, and divine authority. The rock has already been placed. It is God himself, his eternal truth, his unconditional love. My task is simply to anchor myself in it day after day, thought after thought, choice after choice. I do not fear if some storm strike my life. The house built on rock may be struck, but it will not be destroyed. It may be shaken, but it will not fall. And me who build on the rock will remain, and my inner throne, sustained by grace, will be unshakable. And my life anchored in the truth will be a living testimony of God's faithfulness. So I build today. I build in the spirit. I build on the rock and nothing, absolutely nothing will ever move me. 

Chapter 13
   
Praise as a shield. The hidden power of gratitude to overcome fear. 

The soul that has learned to praise lives in a different sphere, one that is inaccessible to the poison of fear. Where there is sincere praise, fear cannot take root. Where there is living gratitude, the mental attack slides off like water on polished stone. Most people try to overcome fear with arguments, with efforts of will, with distractions or with resistance. But the most powerful way, the real way is praise. Because praise transforms the internal atmosphere, elevates the mind, strengthens the heart, and reorders the entire vibration of the being, aligning it with the higher currents of divine life. What is praise in its purest essence? Praise is not just singing hymns or speaking nice words. True praise is an act of spiritual recognition. It is seeing God in everything and blessing God's presence. It is declaring even when the senses say otherwise, I am here. I am working. I am good and my good prevails. When I praise, I detach from myself. I stop revolving around my fears, doubts, and shortcomings. I look beyond appearances and connect with reality. And in that connection, fear loses its power because fear needs my attention to survive. Praise cuts off its food. For praise acts as an invincible shield. In the moment I praise, I affirm the good is more real than the evil. I affirm that God is in control even though my mind may not understand it. I affirm that light is stronger than any shadow. Thus, instead of fighting fear, which often only strengthens it, I transcend it. I rise above its field of influence, not denying its existence, but affirming a greater reality. The scriptures are full of this teaching. Sing to the Lord, for he has done marvelous things. Give thanks in everything, for this is the will of God for you. I not give thanks only when everything goes well, but in everything. Because the act of praising does not depend on external circumstances, but on the internal decision to live in truth. How to practice praise as a shield against fear? First, by making gratitude a daily discipline. I do not wait to feel gratitude to give thanks. I begin thanking and the right feelings will follow. Each morning before I begin my activities, I say, "Thank you, Father, for your presence in my life. Thank you for even without seeing the whole way, I know I am in your hands. Thank you for your good manifesting in me, through me and around me." And each night before I sleep, I review mentally the blessings of the day, from the greatest to the seemingly insignificant, every protected step, every kind word received, every moment of peace, the mind trained in gratitude has no time or space for fear. Second, by using active praise in moments of mental attack. When I feel fear looming, I do not engage in a discussion with it. I praise, I give thanks, I declare, I praise you, my God, because you are greater than any fear. I praise you because your light dispels every shadow. I praise you because nothing can separate me from your love. This is a spiritual response of the highest vibration, so high that the fear which feeds on low vibrations cannot sustain itself in its presence. Third, remembering that praise does not change God, it changes me. God does not need to be reminded of his goodness. It is me who needs to remember in every fiber of my being that I live in a universe governed by love and not by chance. That my life is not a solitary struggle but a continuous expression of the one life. I remember this deeply. No matter how dark a situation seems, there is always something I can praise, always something you can be thankful for. And if I can't see anything yet, I can praise the very truth that God is, that God works, that God loves. Praise is not mere wax. It is spiritual vision, seeing beyond the visible. It is holding on to truth while appearances change. When I make praise my habitual atmosphere, fear becomes strange to my being. It no longer finds an echo in my consciousness. It can no longer germinate in my inner field. Then I walk through trials with my head held high. I speak words of faith when others remain silent in fear. I hold my peace when the world trembles and I silently become a living testimony to the power of God. Not because I have never known fear, but because I have learned to overcome it with the highest weapon, praise. So I praise today. I praise when I understand and when I don't understand. I praise on the mountaintop and in the valley. I praise in abundance and in apparent lack. Each word of gratitude I speak will be another brick in the wall that protects my inner throne. Each act of praise will be a shield that deflects the dart of fear. And I will live not as one who fears losing their good, but as one who knows their good is secured in God and that nothing can take it away. So it is. So it has always been and so it will be eternally. 

Chapter 14
   
The fall as a leap towards heaven. How to transform any stumble into a greater ascent? 

On the path of spiritual life, one of the greatest mistakes is to believe that a fall means defeat. We have been subtly or explicitly taught to fear mistakes, to be ashamed of stumbling, to think that any deviation irreparably separates me from our destiny. But in God's vision, every fall can be, if I choose it, a leap towards heaven. The mature soul does not measure its life by the absence of falls, but by how it rises from them. True growth is not about never falling, but about learning to use every fall as a stepping stone to a higher consciousness. Just as a child learns to walk by falling and getting back up, so the soul learns to reign, falling at times, but always returning to its center, strengthened, enlightened, wiser. The fall is not punishment. The fall is opportunity. The fall is a lesson. The fall is momentum. What truly happens when I fall? Falling does not mean losing God's favor. God does not change. His love, his calling, his provision remain intact, unaltered. To fall means that at some point (9) I acted from the old mind, from fear, from impatience, from pride, from disconnection. But that does not annul the divine plan. That does not erase the work God is doing in me. That does not cancel my right to reign. Every fall contains within it the seed of a greater ascent. Every mistake seen in the light of the spirit reveals areas where consciousness needs to mature, to cleanse, to refine its vibration. The problem is not falling. The problem is staying fallen. It's settling into guilt, self-pity, hopelessness. This is why it is vital to know how to transform a fall into ascent. How can I do it? First, by recognizing the mistake without condemnation. True recognition is not self flagellation, not sinking into guilt. It's seeing clearly without excuses, without justifications, without masks. Here at this point, I strayed from love. Here I acted from fear and not from faith. Here I momentarily forgot who I am, not to punish myself, but to reclaim my power, because power can only be exercised in truth, and truth begins with an honest look at oneself. Second, by immediately forgiving myself. Just as God doesn't keep a record of my falls, neither should I. Forgiveness doesn't minimize the mistake, it dissolves it. Forgiveness doesn't deny the fall, it redeems it. I say to myself with all the spiritual authority of my inner spirit, I forgive myself for forgetting my truth. I rise now in the name of the spirit within me. This fall is a springboard to a greater faith, to a purer love, to a higher life. Third, by drawing the living lesson. Every fall carries with it a hidden gift, a lesson that if received will empower me to hold greater blessings. I ask myself, what can I learn from this experience? What inner vulnerability was revealed that I can now strengthen? Which part of my consciousness needs more light, more love, more discipline, not as an inquisitor, but as a loving gardener who observes his field and patiently removes the weeds with hope and care. Fourth, by renewing my commitment to my divine purpose. The fall doesn't change my calling. It doesn't alter my destiny. It only reveals areas where I must assert myself more consciously. I declare with strength, "Nothing separates me from the love of God. Nothing can stop the perfect work God is doing in me. I rise. I continue. I advance from glory to glory." I remember this deeply. The great men and women of God are not those who never fell. They are the ones who every time they fell got up stronger. They are the ones who transformed each stumble into a stepping stone. They are the ones who learned to see even in tears, the seeds of a future filled with light. The fall does not have the final word. Grace does. The fall does not define my story. Redemption does. The fall is not my end. It is the momentum that can launch me higher than I ever dreamed. Just as the seed must fall to the ground to germinate, some of my apparent defeats are only preparations for a more glorious resurrection. Today, I can choose. I can see my mistakes as condemnation or as a school. I can see my stumbles as failure or as momentum. I can see my fall as death or as the precursor to a greater flight. God does not abandon me in the fall. God holds me, calls me, lifts me. God sees beyond the dust on my knees. He sees the eagle I am destined to be. So I rise up, I shake off the dust of old guilt. I lift my eyes to the horizon and keep ascending. Every redeemed fall will become a crown. Every healed wound will be a testimony of his faithfulness. Every tear transformed will become a river of blessings. For I am not created to remain on the ground. I am created to soar, to rain, to live in the splendor of divine fullness. So it is. So it has always been, and so it will be eternally. 

Chapter 15

My victory is irrevocable. The final decree. 

The spiritual life has a radiant goal. The full awakening of the consciousness of the inner spirit. This awakening is not a gift that is given and taken away capriciously. It is not an uncertain opportunity or a fragile privilege. It is a definitive victory. Once my soul has remembered its true nature, once it has set its feet on the firm rock of truth, that victory is irrevocable. Nothing and no one can take from me what God has given me. Neither past mistakes nor present fears, nor future circumstances have real power over the good that has been established in me by divine decree. The holy spirit expresses this clearly. My sheep hear my voice and I know them and they follow me and I give them eternal life and they shall never perish. Neither shall anyone pluck them out of my hand. This is the eternal reality that I must engrave deep in my being. I am in the hands of God and the hands of God do not fail, do not falter, do not abandon. What does it mean that my victory is irrevocable? It means that the authentic good I have spiritually conquered cannot be lost. External manifestations may change as the seasons change, as the waves rise and fall. But the consciousness I have established, the inner throne I have built remains. For true victory is not external. It does not depend on a house, a job, a relationship, or applause. True victory is internal. It is mastery over myself. It is conscious union with God. It is the certainty of my spiritual identity. When I know who I am, it doesn't matter what comes. It doesn't matter what others say. It doesn't matter if the winds blow or the waters roar. I remain. I reign. I live from a center that cannot be shaken. What to do to seal this victory in my consciousness? First, establish the final decree in my mind and heart. What God has given me, no one can take away. My victory is eternal because it is the manifestation of God's will in me. I do not repeat these words as a mere ritual. I feel their truth. I allow every word to permeate my soul to vibrate in every cell of my being. Second, I let go of all worry about the how and when. The victorious soul does not obsess over the details of manifestation. It knows that the good has already been decreed in heaven and in due time it will be reflected on earth. I trust the process. I trust the love that guides me. I trust the life that sustains me. Third, I live each day as a legitimate heir of the kingdom. Not as a beggar waiting for spiritual handouts. Not as a fugitive fearing to lose what has been conquered, but as a beloved child, secure in my position, calm in my power, serene in my soul. What attitudes does someone who knows their victory is irrevocable cultivate? Peace that does not depend on appearances. Gratitude that does not wait for ideal conditions. Generosity that fears no lack. Joy that does not fade in the face of trials. Faith that moves through shadows without wavering. I remember this deeply. God does not play with my destiny. God does not give me a blessing today to take it away tomorrow. God does not regret calling me, choosing me, crowning me with his glory. Faithful is he who promised, who also performs. My part is to believe. My part is to remain. My part is to live each moment as one who knows the victory has already been secured. And if at any moment I feel my faith shaking, if the old mind whispers doubts, if circumstances seem to contradict my truth, I do not be alarmed. I simply return to the rock. I reaffirm the decree. My victory is irrevocable. I'm in the hands of God. Nothing and no one can take me out of his love. Today, I declare over my life, the victory I have begun to glimpse will be complete. The seed I swed in tears I will reap in joy. The throne I have built within me will remain firm. And from there I will rule my world with wisdom, with love, with peace. I am not at the mercy of chance. I am not in the hands of error. I am not exposed to losing my good. And I am sealed with the seal of the spirit. I am anointed to reign in life. I am destined to live in the eternal fullness of inner spirit within me. So I walk with a firm step. I speak with a clear voice. I love with a free heart. For my victory is not an uncertain dream. It is an eternal decree. It is a done deal. It is the inevitable expression of God's perfect work in me. So it is. So it has been since the beginning. And so it will be forever and ever.
