Chapter 12

The Key and the Kingdom. Living without conflicts.

Everything I have read so far has prepared me for this. To live in a state of consciousness where the golden key is no longer an emergency resource in the face of conflict, but a way of life that dissolves conflict before it even appears. It is no longer about applying the key as if searching for it in a drawer when something becomes complicated, but about integrating it so deeply that my mind operates from it naturally and continuously like a stabilized spiritual habit. This is the true goal that the thought of God becomes my inner atmosphere. When this occurs, the kingdom of God manifests as order, peace, and guidance in every aspect of my human experience. Living without conflicts does not mean challenges will not arise. It means my mind no longer identifies them as problems. I have stopped reacting and have begun responding from truth. I have left behind the ego's automatic interpretation of every event as a threat and have entered the supremacy of the spirit which recognizes every situation as an expression of divine order in the process of unfolding. In this state, I do not apply the key to solve something. I apply it because it is my way of thinking because I no longer know how to live otherwise. I have discovered that thinking of God is like breathing life. I express this with perfect clarity in an image that encapsulates the core of this teaching. I wait quietly at the threshold of your heart. If you welcome me in, I will share my presence with you, and we will walk together in peace. The inner spirit is always calling within me. It does not shout nor impose. It calls. Its presence is constant but it requires openness. That door is my attention. Wherever I place my attention, I choose who enters my mind. The problem or the inner spirit, fear or truth, the world's disorder or the kingdom of God. The golden key is precisely that conscious openness that says, "Enter the kingdom and reign in me." This chapter does not offer another technique. It offers a vision, a radically different way of conceiving my day, my life, my being. It is about living preventively, not reactively. Not waiting for conflict to remind me of the truth, but establishing truth as the platform for every thought. Just as a house built on solid foundations does not fear wind or rain. A consciousness founded on principle does not tremble before circumstances, because its peace does not depend on the external, but flows from the center. How is this state of consciousness established? Not through accumulating knowledge, but through silent daily faithful practice. Over and over again as I awaken in the morning before my thoughts scatter into the day's tasks, I repeat in my mind, "God is my life. God is my guide. God is my substance." Let this idea not be an empty affirmation, but a vibration that permeates everything. Then, as I walk, work, converse, and decide, maintain this focus. I do not need to repeat phrases constantly. I simply keep my attention turned toward the good. I do not be carried away by the noise of the world. I do not give my energy to what I do not wish to manifest. I remain, persevere, breathe with God and everything else will align naturally. This state of life is not achieved through effort but through inner surrender. I am not called to sustain the thought of God as one carries a weight. I am called to be sustained by it. The kingdom is not something I create. It is something I allow. It is like the sun. I do not make it nor control it. I only remove the clouds that obscure it. And those clouds are my old thoughts. Fear, doubt, control, resistance. Each time I think of God, I remove a cloud. And when the atmosphere is clear, the kingdom manifests. A useful practice to cultivate this state is the metaphysical visualization of the kingdom. I spend a few minutes each day closing my eyes and seeing in my mind something higher. I visualize a city of perfect light where everything vibrates in harmony. I feel that I inhabit this city. Everything is in order. No one rushes. No one fears. Decisions flow with wisdom. Relationships are clear and loving. The body is healthy, strong and peaceful. In this city, only God governs. Then upon opening my eyes, I remember that this city is my own elevated consciousness. It is not far away. It is not a future heaven. It is the kingdom revealed when I think of God. And the more time I spend in this internal atmosphere, the more my external world begins to reflect it. Living this way is not fantasy. It is the result of a re-educated mind, a surrendered will, a heart that has decided never to return to the slavery of fear. Conflict is not mandatory. It is a choice of thought. And when my thought aligns with God, conflict has no space to arise. Even if external events seem difficult, my peace remains intact. And that peace does not only bless me, it blesses all around me. It becomes a contagious atmosphere, a silent presence that elevates. Final reflection. The golden key is not just another tool in my spiritual toolbox. It is the door to the kingdom. It is not something I use. It is something I become. By thinking of God daily, living from Gods presence in the ordinary, choosing Gods peace as my habitual atmosphere, I allow heaven to come to earth through me. This is my true purpose. This is my privilege. A life lived from the kingdom without conflict, without fear, without separation. Silent prayer. Inner spirit, enter my mind and dwell. Make my consciousness your kingdom. I do not wish to live in reaction, but in presence. I do not want to wait for something to break before seeking you. I want to live with you, in you, from you. Grant me the grace to remain. Grant me the joy of remembering you effortlessly. Grant me peace that does not depend on the world. I open the door. You are already calling. Enter and reign. Amen.
